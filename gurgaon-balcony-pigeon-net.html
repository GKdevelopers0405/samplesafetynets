Why Install Pigeon Nets in Your Balcony? 

Picture sipping your coffee in the morning on your balcony without having to chase birds off or clean up their feces. That is the appeal of putting in pigeon nets! By acting as a barrier, these nets keep pigeons out of your balcony area, keeping it tidy and free of birds.

How to install pigeon net in balcony

Pigeon netting is one good way to keep these birds away from your place especially if your balcony is in your house.

1. Measure your balcony: Before making the net, it is essential to measure the length and width of the balcony to establish the size of the net. You should add height measurements too, especially when measuring the area right to the floor or ceiling.

2. Purchase the netting: Go to the nearest supermarket or a store that sells materials and tools and you can ask for pigeon control netting. This is particularly important especially when purchasing the netting material as it should be strong and durable enough to endure the ravages of weather.

3. Prepare the balcony: It is nicer to have a clean balcony without dust, any particles on the floor or pigeons dropping on the space. This will provide a clean environment for the net to be installed.

4. Install hooks or brackets: If your balcony has a specific framework, some of the points mentioned above might necessitate hooks, brackets or other accessories for anchoring of the netting. These should be well fixed on the walls or ceiling of the balcony if they are to be installed there.

5. Attach the netting: Unroll the netting right across the balcony in such a way that it creates a cover over all the area which is to be protected. Make sure that the hooks or brackets you choose to anchor the netting are securely fastened with zip ties or sturdy twine. Ensure that the pigeon net is stretched tightly to enable avoiding strain due to pigeons getting caught in loops.

6. Secure the edges: After completing the netting, fasten the wires by folding them below various goods or using extra hooks or brackets as required. This will discourage the pigeons from accessing the balcony from the sides or corners.

7. Trim excess netting: You can cut away any loose ends, or any netting that hangs free of the cake using scissors or a blade such as a utility knife in order to make it neater.

8. Inspect and test: Once the netting is put in place, it is recommended that one takes his/her time and ensure that there are no shortcomings which the pigeons may possibly use to gain access to the place. Also ensure that the netting has strong enough to support the weight of birds in case they perch on it this can be tested by applying pressure on it.

If you have followed the above outlined procedures, you will have a pigeon net installed in your balcony and no more pigeons.
